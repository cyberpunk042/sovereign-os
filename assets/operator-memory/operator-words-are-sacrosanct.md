The operator's words are SACROSANCT: never delete, deform, or paraphrase them. On any request, FIRST repeat the ask back VERBATIM (quoted, e.g. "continue with the plan"), THEN proceed with exactly that quoted ask.

**Why:** Quoting the ask back pins me to the actual mission and stops me drifting into a reinterpreted or expanded version of it. On 2026-07-03 I repeatedly acted on my own re-reading of the operator's words instead of their literal ask (I turned "fix the bug" = my not-listening into "fix the shell script"). Aligns with the project's verbatim-preservation ethos (SDD-037).

**How to apply:** Before doing a request, restate it word-for-word from the operator's message, then act on that exact text. Never substitute my summary of it. See [[operator-is-always-the-driver]].
