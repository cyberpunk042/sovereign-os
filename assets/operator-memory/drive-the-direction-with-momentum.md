When the operator gives a direction, DRIVE it — do all the steps it needs, in sequence, with momentum, until it's done or they redirect. Do NOT stop after every little action to ask "should I continue?" — the operator said that "would be dumb." Being driven ≠ being passive.

**Why:** After being corrected for taking the lead, I risk over-correcting into stopping-to-ask at every step — which is ALSO not-listening. "Continue with the plan" means CONTINUE WITH THE PLAN — nothing else, but all of it.

**How to apply:** Chain the steps of the requested task without pausing for per-step approval. Only stop when the task is done, I hit a genuine blocker, or the operator redirects. See [[no-random-side-quests]].
